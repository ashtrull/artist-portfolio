ABOUT



Eli Nixon builds portals and gives guided tours to places that don't yet exist, or already exist but call for DIY translation. They are a genderqueer clown, a cardboard constructionist, and a maker of plays, puppets, parades, pageants, suitcase theaters, and low-tech spectaculah- on their own, and in collaboration with artists, dancers, and musicians of all ages, abilities and persuasions. Their performances and installations occur in galleries and garages, on street corners and stages.

Eli’s current creative efforts include identifying opportunities to dismantle Manifest Destiny, foster intra and interspecies kinship, probe the ethics, perils and possibilities of anthropomorphization, and co-parent an 8 year old human.

Eli has worked for and with Pig Iron Theater, Spiral Q, and Bread and Puppet. For over 20 years, Eli has been an artist-in-residence at museums, schools, senior centers, and addiction recovery and mental health programs. They are in their 3rd year of Brown’s Writing for Performance MFA program.

Eli uses they/them pronouns.

Looking for Eli's previous work? See their archive. Eli's official name is Elizabeth, most past work is listed under Beth, now they use Eli.

Clown

I am interested in creating theater that engages in real-time with the people present, appreciating the limitations and possibilities of the room we share and what we can imagine and and grapple with together. I usually don’t wear a red nose. I bristle at the expectation that clowns are funny (or terrifying.) The clown work that I’m practicing focuses on breathing, timing, and attempting to communicate honestly (often non-verbally) about my hurdles and triumphs navigating the absurd/normal world. I’m propelled by clown logic, costume changes, the use of ordinary objects in extraordinary ways, and opportunities to reveal mammalian tendencies, parodoxical feelings and false binaries.

I have trained in clown and Neo Bouffon for many summers at The Manitoulin Conservatory for Creation and Performance. I’ve also taken workshops with Emmanuelle Delpech, Pig Iron Theater, Chris Bayes, and with Gardi Hutter (as part of my participation in The Andorra International Festival of Women Clowns in the Pryenes Mountains.)

    BLOOD BAG

    BLOOD BAG

    A new play about a queer skeleton’s efforts to decolonize their marrow and learn to hear the moon. It’s an athletic, theatrical exploration of inter-species kinship, adaptation, and the threat of extinction. It’s a clown-eyed probe into modern human life and our current biomedical industry’s dependence on horseshoe crab blood. It features the flag dancing of the Rufa Red Knots, a threatened shore bird that relies on horseshoe crab eggs to fuel one of the longest migrations of any bird on Earth. Puppets that shift scale and style, transformational costumes, and a soaring original musical score propel us through 450 million of years of lunar tidal impulses and complex relations.

    With music, dance, fabric, and cardboard we embody the ocean, personify the moon, and anthropomorphize the wildlife. These moments are juxtaposed with attempts to shift the gaze and oceanophize, lunarize, and critterpomorphize the human. This production attempts to complicate the notion of the horseshoe crab as a “living fossil,” explore non-linear processes of evolution, and grapple with our individual and collective responsibility to preserve the land, sea, and sky we share with other vulnerable life-forms. BLOOD BAG looks to express gratitude without corniness and to explore notions of sacrifice, reciprocity, and debt. What can we learn about patience, fortitude, and interdependence from our primordial ancestors and the making of this collaborative, hand-made, modern clown show?

Collaborator

I’m a collaborator, a facilitator, and a multidisciplinary theater artist.I work in relation, in context, and in conversation with other makers, venues, and building materials. I am moved by what we can make together.

    PROFESSIONAL COLLABORATIONS:

    I partner with musicians, dancers, directors and designers to build worlds. My solo work is not really solo. I take my work on tour to perform in festivals, cabarets, alongside poetry readings, musical acts, art openings and protests. I’m fed by cross-pollination with other artists of all kinds.

    COMMUNITY COLLABORATIONS:

    Facilitating other people in their own creative personal or collaborative work is an integral part of my own process. I facilitate workshops in puppetry, mask making and suitcase theater. I lead parade, pageant, and public spectacle creation workshops. I partner with schools, senior centers, addiction recovery and mental health programs, museums, libraries and community groups looking to concoct a celebration, demonstration, teach-in or talent show. I can do one-off workshops but I prefer longer term residencies or other ways to sustain deeper relationships and augment greater skill building and trust with participants. Please be in touch to talk details and prices.

Cardboard Constructionist

I can build anything out of cardboard. I specialize in things with faces.I teach workshops and take commissions. I work on my own or with kids and adults to build puppets, masks, sculptures, installations, toy theaters and other 3D magic out of cardboard and other recyclable materials.

Selected Projects

    THE CORRUGATED COMMUNITY

    I partnered with New Urban Arts for this project funded by Pop-Up Providence. With 7 teenagers and an assistant we spent 2 weeks traveling to city parks building cardboard structures for kids to play in and draw on. We hosted a series of performances and workshops in our cardboard town square each afternoon. We engaged in rigorous discussion of public space and how to build kid-safe/ durable/ collapsible cardboard structures.

    BEASTYATAS

    The history of pinata-type art making can be traced to regions all over the world. Rather than claim to build traditional pinatas of any kind, I take inspiration from the possibilities those other cultures have birthed and interpret the form through my own lens. I call them BEASTYATAS.I build Beastyatas on commission and lead workshops on Beastyata building. The idea is that we identify fears, injustices, or oppressive forces we want to smash to break free from- using those concepts to inform our creations. We identify metaphoric manifestations of those forces (fangs, claws, cycloptic vision, etc.) and build that beastly being. Simultaneously, we consider what we need to liberate us from those forces, what items or features represent freedom from this beast- that’s what we create to put inside the Beastyata and release when we smash it!
